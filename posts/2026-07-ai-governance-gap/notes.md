# AI Pilots vs Production — Governance Gap

**Date:** 2026-07-02  
**Format:** Single terminal-style slide (1200×627)  
**Topic:** Why AI pilots fail differently than AI production

## Hook

88% of enterprise AI pilots never reach production — but the failure mode changes once you're live.

## Key points

- **Pilot:** limited scope, manual review, question is model accuracy
- **Governance gap:** ownership undefined — who reviews, pauses, fixes, communicates?
- **Production:** real users, no buffer, question becomes "who owns the impact?"
- Only 7% of orgs still piloting are confident they'd pass an AI governance audit in 90 days

## Caption draft

AI pilots don't fail the same way AI production does.

In a pilot, the question is: "Is the model accurate enough?"

In production, the question becomes: "Who owns the impact when it doesn't behave as expected?"

88% of enterprise AI pilots never reach production (McKinsey/CIO, 2026).
Only 7% still in pilot mode are confident they'd pass an AI governance audit in 90 days (Grant Thornton 2026).

Before you scale: define the human review, escalation, and support process — not just the model.
