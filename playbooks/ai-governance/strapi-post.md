# AI Pilots Don't Fail Like Production — The Governance Gap Nobody Defines

Most enterprise AI programs die in a gap nobody names.

In pilot, everyone asks: *"Is the model accurate enough?"*

In production, the question changes: *"Who owns the impact when it doesn't behave as expected?"*

That shift is not a technical problem. It is a **governance gap** — and most organizations walk into it without defining four roles: who reviews, who pauses, who fixes, who communicates to the business.

## The numbers

- **88%** of enterprise AI pilots never reach production (CIO / McKinsey, 2026)
- Only **7%** of orgs still piloting say they could pass an AI governance audit in 90 days
- **74%** of fully integrated AI orgs are confident on governance audit readiness

The difference is not model quality. It is whether ownership was defined **before** go-live.

## What to do before you scale

1. **Human review** — Who signs off on AI outputs before they reach users?
2. **Escalation** — Who can pause the system when it misbehaves?
3. **Remediation** — Who coordinates the fix?
4. **Communication** — Who tells the business what happened?

Skip these and you have a working model with a broken operating model.
